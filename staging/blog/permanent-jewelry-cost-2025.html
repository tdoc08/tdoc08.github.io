What Factors Affect the Price of Permanent Jewelry?

Permanent jewelry isn't just a chain — it's a skilled service performed on your body. Here's what goes into the price:

1. Metal Type and Quality

The single biggest factor in permanent jewelry pricing is the metal. Higher-quality metals cost more, but they also perform better on your skin and last longer without losing their appearance.

  • Sterling silver is the most affordable option. It's 92.5% pure silver, hypoallergenic, and a beautiful choice for everyday wear. It can tarnish over time, especially in humid climates or with frequent water exposure, but it polishes up easily.
  • 14k gold filled is our most popular option. It has a thick layer of real 14k gold bonded to a base metal core, giving you the look and feel of solid gold at a fraction of the price. It doesn't tarnish, won't flake or peel, and holds up extremely well with daily wear.
  • Solid gold (14k or 18k) is the premium choice. It's the most durable, the most hypoallergenic, and the most valuable. Pricing fluctuates with the gold market and is quoted individually.

2. Chain Length

Longer pieces require more material, which is why anklets, necklaces, and hand chains cost more than bracelets. Necklace and hand chain pricing also reflects the additional fit time involved in placing the piece correctly.

3. The Weld Itself

The micro-weld that closes your piece is performed by a trained jewelry specialist using a precision pulse arc welder. The jewelry is sized to fit exactly on your body — no clasps, no slipping, no adjusting. This is a skilled service, not just a retail purchase.

4. Chain Style

Not all chains are priced the same. Delicate cable chains are our standard starting price. Specialty styles — paperclip, box chain, figaro, curb — may vary slightly depending on availability. Ask at your appointment about what styles are in stock.

Gold Filled vs. Sterling Silver: Which Is Worth It?

This is the most common question we get. Both are excellent permanent jewelry metals, but they suit different people and priorities.

14k Gold Filled — $95+ Most Popular

  • Warm gold tone that doesn't tarnish
  • Thick gold layer — won't peel or flake
  • Excellent for sensitive skin
  • Safe for daily showers, swimming, gym
  • Virtually indistinguishable from solid gold visually
  • Best for those who want gold look + longevity

Sterling Silver — $85+ Best Value

  • Cool, classic silver tone
  • 92.5% pure silver, hypoallergenic
  • May tarnish over time with humidity/water
  • Easy to polish back to bright finish
  • Slightly lower entry price
  • Best for those who prefer silver or want lowest starting price

Both metals are completely safe for permanent wear and can last for years with normal care. The choice really comes down to your personal style preference — warm gold tones or cool silver tones — and how much upkeep you're comfortable with.

Our honest take: For most clients, gold filled is worth the extra $10. You get a piece that looks bright and golden indefinitely without any maintenance. Sterling silver is a beautiful choice too, especially if you love the silver look and don't mind the occasional polish.

What About Solid Gold?

Solid gold permanent jewelry is the top tier — and for good reason. When you choose solid gold, you're investing in a piece that:

  • Will never tarnish, discolor, or change appearance
  • Is completely hypoallergenic (ideal for those with metal sensitivities)
  • Has real intrinsic metal value
  • Can be melted down or reworked by a jeweler if ever removed
  • Comes in 14k (more durable) or 18k (higher gold content, deeper color)

Because gold prices fluctuate with commodity markets, we quote solid gold pieces at market rate at the time of your appointment rather than publishing a fixed price. If you're interested in solid gold, reach out ahead of your visit so we can give you a current estimate.

Is Permanent Jewelry a Good Value?

Compared to traditional fine jewelry, permanent jewelry is an excellent value — especially when you consider what you're getting:

  • A custom-fit piece. Your jewelry is sized and welded to your exact measurements. There's no standard sizing — it's made for your body.
  • No clasp to break or lose. Traditional bracelets and anklets regularly lose clasps or get snagged. Permanent jewelry has no clasp at all — it just stays on.
  • A lasting wearable memory. Many clients get permanent jewelry to mark a milestone, a friendship, or a moment they want to carry with them. That's a different kind of value than a retail bracelet.
  • A professional service experience. You're not just buying a chain — you're getting it welded on by a trained specialist in a curated studio setting.

For context: a high-quality fine jewelry bracelet from a traditional retailer can run $200–$500+ for similar metal quality. Permanent jewelry, starting at $85, is genuinely a bargain for what it is.

Frequently Asked Questions About Permanent Jewelry Pricing

Is there a deposit or cancellation fee?

No deposit is required when you book. Cancellations and reschedules are free — just give us reasonable advance notice so we can offer your slot to someone else.

Can I add a charm to my piece?

Yes, we carry charm add-ons that can be welded or hung directly on your permanent piece. Charm pricing varies — ask at your appointment for current availability and pricing.

What's included in the price?

Everything. The chain, the weld, the fit, and the experience. There are no hidden fees or add-ons. The price you see is all-in.

Do you offer group discounts?

We don't offer per-piece discounts, but we do offer private event bookings for groups — bachelorettes, birthdays, corporate events, and more. A private booking means exclusive access to the studio, a dedicated stylist, and a curated experience for your group. Submit an inquiry to learn more.

Does permanent jewelry hold its value?

Gold filled and sterling silver pieces don't carry significant resale value on their own — they're small in weight and bonded materials. Solid gold pieces do have metal value and could theoretically be melted or repurposed by a jeweler. Most clients think of permanent jewelry as a wearable experience rather than an investment piece.
