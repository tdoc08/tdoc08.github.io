Gulf Coast Guide

Alabama's
Gulf Coast

Gulf Shores consistently ranks among the top beach destinations in the Southeast — and for good reason. The water is warm, the beaches are breathtaking, and there's far more to do than just sit in the sand. Whether you're here for a weekend getaway, a bachelorette trip, a family vacation, or a Gulf Coast adventure, this guide covers the best the area has to offer.

The Outdoors

Beach &
Nature

Gulf Shores Public Beach

Miles of white quartz-sand beach and clear emerald-green Gulf water. The main public beach area on West Beach Blvd has parking, restrooms, and a boardwalk. Come early on summer weekends.

Gulf State Park

2,644 acres of preserved coastline with a 6,150-foot fishing pier, hiking trails, a resort lodge, and beachfront camping. The pier at sunset is one of the most beautiful views on the Gulf Coast.

Fort Morgan State Historic Site

A beautifully preserved 1834 fort at the tip of the peninsula, guarding Mobile Bay. Walk the battlements and take in dramatic coastal views — about 20 minutes west of Gulf Shores.

Kayaking & Paddleboarding

The back bays offer calm, protected water perfect for kayaking and eco-tours — a great way to spot dolphins, herons, and stingrays up close.

Dining & Nightlife

Food &
Fun

Fresh Gulf Seafood

Some of the freshest shrimp, crab, oysters, and fish on the Gulf Coast. Restaurants along Fort Morgan Road serve locally caught seafood daily — don't leave without trying Gulf shrimp.

The Hangout Music Festival & Venue

A massive beachfront restaurant and entertainment complex with live music year-round, a pool, and one of the best views in Gulf Shores. Annual festival in May brings national headliners.

Foley & The Outlet Strip

Just 10 minutes north: Tanger Outlets, The OWA amusement park, dozens of restaurants, and our Evermore boutique studio. Great for a rainy-day alternative to the beach.

More to Explore

Family &
Day Trips

The OWA (Foley)

A family entertainment district with a full amusement park, restaurants, and shopping — about 10 minutes north of Gulf Shores. Perfect for a day off the beach with kids.

Alabama Gulf Coast Zoo

"The Little Zoo That Could" — a small but diverse zoo with lions, tigers, bears, and exotic birds. Walkable, intimate, and great for kids. Near Hwy 59.

Orange Beach (5 min east)

Beautiful beaches, world-class fishing charters, The Wharf entertainment complex, and great restaurants. Easy to combine with a Gulf Shores day.

Pensacola, FL (45 min east)

Powder-white sand beaches, the National Naval Aviation Museum (free & world-class), historic downtown, and excellent dining and craft beer scenes.

Mobile, AL (1 hr west)

The USS Alabama Battleship Memorial Park, the beautiful historic district, and the Mobile Carnival Museum — Mardi Gras was actually founded in Mobile, not New Orleans.
